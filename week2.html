Over the past year, most of us have been using Claude or ChatGPT the same way: opening a chat, typing a question, getting an answer. That's a good start. But it's a fraction of what's possible.

Before we go further, let's name what we're actually doing when we use these tools. There are 7 core things you can do with AI chat tools.

What you can do with AI Chat tools
Explain. Understand something complex quickly. A concept, a tool, a market, or a decision someone else made.
Research. Gather relevant information on a topic to inform your work.
Synthesize. Extract the key meaning or takeaways from one or more sources.
Analyze. Find patterns, anomalies, or insights in data or information.
Create. Generate something net new: a draft, a plan, a concept, or an asset.
Polish. Improve something that already exists. Tighten it, reformat it, rewrite it, or adapt it for a different audience.
Decide. Work through a choice by pressure-testing options, tradeoffs, or recommendations.
Most people live in Explain, Create, and Polish. The team that wins is the one that uses all seven intentionally.
Claude Cowork: Take Action

There's an eighth thing AI can do: act. Send a message. Update a record. Pull a report. Interact with the tools you use every day.

The problem is that Chat alone can't do this reliably. Claude doesn't know what's in your Slack, your ad accounts, or your campaign dashboard unless you tell it every single time. That's where Connectors come in.

Connectors link Claude directly to your software. When Claude is connected to a tool, it can read from it, write to it, and act inside it without you copying and pasting anything. It turns Take action from a nice idea into something that actually works.

This is why Cowork exists. Chat is powerful for thinking. Cowork is powerful for doing, and for doing it repeatedly and automatically without you having to press go every time.

Cowork: three things Chat can't do
1. Skills — make any process repeatable
The signal: you've run the same Claude prompt more than twice.
A Skill in Cowork is basically just a reusable prompt with instructions. You build it once and Claude runs the same process the same way every time. No rebuilding, no re-explaining, no variance.
Example
Every week you summarize a performance report and pull out key takeaways for your team. Instead of starting from scratch each time, you build a Skill that takes the report as input and outputs a formatted summary in the same structure, every time.
2. Schedule — remove yourself from the equation
Schedule lets you automate any process based on time or an event. You can jump straight into Schedule without building a Skill first. Just set up the instructions and the timing together in one place, and it runs without you.
The goal this week is to find a real workflow that uses Schedule with a Connector you already have. Something Claude can pull from or push to automatically. That's where Schedule becomes genuinely powerful.
Example with Connector
Every Friday at 9am, Claude pulls your team's weekly OKRs from Google Drive, summarizes progress, flags anything off track, and posts it to Slack. You didn't touch it.
If you can't find a productive use case with a Connector this week
Scheduled research. Set up a recurring digest on something relevant to your role, function, or vertical. Example: every Monday morning, Claude sends you a summary of the top new AI tools for video production.
Scheduled competitive intel. Use the ATN MCP to run a weekly pull on a competitor, vertical, or ad trend you care about and deliver a summary to Slack.
3. Alert — know before you have to ask
Alert is Schedule with a condition. Instead of running on a fixed cadence, it triggers when something specific happens and tells you about it. This requires a Connector.
Example
Claude monitors your campaign performance. When ROAS drops below a threshold, it sends you a Gmail alert before you've even opened your dashboard.
A note on how far Claude's reach actually goes

Claude can connect to nearly any service — not just the Connectors available in Cowork today. Beyond Connectors, Claude can integrate with custom APIs, internal tools, and workflows built with Claude Code. This week we're focused on Connectors as your starting point. We'll cover additional integration types in the weeks ahead.
