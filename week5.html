Introduction

Over the past few weeks, we've come a long way. We've learned how to connect Claude to our tools, have it complete actions on our behalf, and automate those actions so they happen without us lifting a finger. We've learned how to keep leveling up by following the public conversation around AI. And the center of gravity for most of that work has been Cowork -an incredible copilot for nearly any business user.

But there's one more dimension that makes AI tools significantly more powerful, and it's the one we're tackling this week: memory. When an AI has persistent context about who you are, what you're working on, and how you like to work -and when that context is reinforced automatically over time -the quality of its thinking and output goes up dramatically. Memory is the difference between an AI that you have to re-explain yourself to every single conversation, and one that actually knows you.

Using Projects to Unlock Memory

Most people have that one chat thread they keep coming back to. The one where all the context already lives -the new channel you're launching, your new objective for this sprint, your 1:1s with your manager. They go back to that thread not because it's organized, but because starting a new one means re-explaining everything. That's a workaround, not a workflow.

Projects are built for this. At its core, memory in Claude works through two mechanisms: instructions (tell Claude who it is, how to behave, and what it should always know) and files (documents, briefs, reference material that Claude can draw from). Combine those with the natural context that builds up across your conversations inside a project, and you get something much closer to a knowledgeable collaborator than a blank slate.

The best use cases aren't abstract -they're the things you're already doing: client accounts, ad channels you manage, specific products or campaigns, performance reviews, personal growth and development, recurring team initiatives.

Why this matters

Projects are your first real taste of a document system -a structured way of injecting persistent context into an AI's world. As you move toward more advanced use (APIs, agents, automation), you'll be building these systems yourself from scratch. Understanding why they work, and what makes them powerful, is the foundation everything else builds on.
